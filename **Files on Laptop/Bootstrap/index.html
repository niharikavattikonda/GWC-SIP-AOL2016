Global Warming and Polar Bears


While global warming is a problem that affects the entire world, the polar ice caps are disproportionately affected, leaving polar bears in persistent danger due as the ice, their home, melts away.

The Plight of Polar Bears

Polar bear diets are struggling as seals are being affected by climate change, and fish are regularly tainted with mercury.

Arctic ice declines of 13.4% per decade force bears to swim for long periods of time between ice floes, causing additional strain on their weights.

Only about 25,000 polar bears are left in the wild, and even as a protected endangered species, the population will likely decline by 30% by 2050.


There are fewer than 25,000 polar bears left in the wild, according to the nonprofit organization Polar Bears International. Near the southern Beaufort Sea, for instance, the population has dropped about 40 percent between 2001 to 2010, from 1,500 to 900 bears.

The Challenges Faced by Polar Bears

Polar bears are in danger because...

  • Annual ice-free periods, which may last up to 5 months by 2050, prevent polar bears from being able to hunt,
  • Warmer temperatures increase the spread of disease among polar bears,
  • Pollution, human encroachment, and resource (oil) exploitation are at peak levels,
  • Polar bears are forced south towards Canada and human contact because of melting ice,
  • Polar bear hunting hasn't yet been outlawed in Greenland, where bear skins are prized,
  • Seals, which are their typical prey, are dying out as well, so polar bears are forced to eat unusual food (even human food), and
  • Global warming is continuing to eat away at their habitats.


21 Ways to Save Polar Bears and Arctic Sea Ice

  1. Reduce, Reuse, Recycle!
  2. Turn off lights when not in use.
  3. Plant a tree or garden.
  4. Prevent litter and collect trash.
  5. Use reusable water bottles and bags.
  6. Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transportation.
  7. Save water and take shorter showers.
  8. Unplug appliances when not in use.
  9. Turn the thermostat down in the winter and up in the summer.
  10. Use rechargeable batteries.
  11. Buy local and organic produce.
  12. Stop air and heating leaks in your house.
  13. Donate to conservation and rescue organizations.


The polar bear is the proverbial "canary in the coal mine" of the serious threat global warming poses to wildlife species around the world, unless we take immediate and significant action to reduce global warming pollution.
