Niharika Vattikonda



Hi there! My name is Niharika Vattikonda and I'm a junior (and aspiring computer scientist!) at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology. I've studied computer science in school through Java classes, but for the most part, I taught myself different languages through Codecademy and other websites - I taught myself HTML, CSS, and PHP. Girls Who Code's Summer Immersion Program has helped me learn even more languages, including Python and Javascript. I'm looking towards learning Ruby and Haskell next. This year, I was designated as the National Winner (Certificate of Distinction) of the National Center for Women and Information Technology's Aspirations in Computing competition.

Beyond academics, I participate in a variety of computing-related activities, including serving the community of girls in STEM through outreach and mentorship. I am an active member of Coding Lady Colonials, the female computing club at my school. I was also one of the earliest members of the CSterhood, a mentorship program the connects younger girls to experienced mentors in CS classes. In addition, I have developed my skills in web development in through building and redesigning web sites (for my Model UN team and Coding Lady Colonials).

Outside of school, I am the Events Director of Inspiring Femgineers, a student-run nonprofit that encourages young girls to pursue STEM fields, with an emphasis on coding through activities such as Mother-Daughter Hack Day. This June, I was invited to attend the White House's United State of Women Summit to contribute to the dialogue surrounding the issues facing women and girls in STEM. In the future, I would like to attend a college that embraces the startup culture, majoring in computer science and business so that I can one day be the CEO of my own startup.

If you're looking to contact me, send me a note!
